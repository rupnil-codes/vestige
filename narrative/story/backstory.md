
## Plot:
> A deeply traumatized man uses hiking as an escape from his deteriorating mind, only to become the source of a tragedy he cannot bear to remember.

### Internal Plot Notes:
```
During childhood the protagonist suffered an extreme trauma --
A devastating house fire that claimed the lives of his family and his dog.
He survived because he was out of town.

He grew up in a Foster home, but he was different --
Due to the extreme psychological scars, he struggled with anxiety, dissociation, and feelings of isolation.
He was shy, observant, and intelligent yet he had a habit of retreating into his own thoughts whenever confronted with stress or trauma.

Therapy took years, he got better but not cured.
As he grew older, he began experiencing occasional memory loses, periods of emotional detachment and paranoia.
These symptoms were manageable enough that they were never fully investigated, and he learned to hide them from others.
His mind was deteriorating but nobody could understand.

To help him cope, therapists tried to engage him into several activities.
Hiking became his favourite.

The wildernes offered him silence, control and distance from people.

Over the years this hobby became his pation and obsession. 
Whenever he got overwhelmed he would dissapear into the forests for hours or days.

Eventually, something happened during one of those trips.
So horrific and terrible that his mind couldnt accept it.

Failing to comprehend the situation, his subconscious did everything to bury the memory.
The fragments of memories remained in nightmares and vague ideas.

Some Years later, he supposedly "became normal" and had a job. Taking a week off from work to hike-- he takes a strange route.
On the less troden route, he goes off course and finds the vestige (remnant) of an abandoned campsite. 
```
